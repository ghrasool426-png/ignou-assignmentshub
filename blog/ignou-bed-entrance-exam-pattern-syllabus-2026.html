IGNOU B.Ed Entrance Test 2026

Complete and official-style guide covering exam pattern, syllabus, marking scheme and key instructions for IGNOU B.Ed January 2026 admission.

What is IGNOU B.Ed Entrance Test?

The IGNOU B.Ed Entrance Test is a national-level examination conducted by the Indira Gandhi National Open University for admission to the Bachelor of Education (B.Ed.) programme. Admission is strictly based on merit in the entrance test, subject to eligibility and seat availability.

IGNOU B.Ed Entrance Test 2026 – Exam Pattern

  • Mode of Examination: Offline (Pen & Paper)
  • Type of Questions: Objective (MCQs)
  • Medium: English and Hindi
  • Total Questions: 100
  • Total Marks: 100
  • Duration: 2 Hours

Section-Wise Question Distribution

Section Subject Area Questions
Part A General English Comprehension 10
Part B General Hindi Comprehension 10
Part C Logical & Analytical Reasoning 20
Part D Educational & General Awareness 25
Part E Teaching-Learning & Subject Competency 35
Total 100

Marking Scheme

  • Correct Answer: +1 mark
  • Negative Marking: No
  • Unattempted Question: 0 marks
Important: There is no negative marking. Candidates are advised to attempt all questions.

IGNOU B.Ed Entrance Test Syllabus 2026

1. General English Comprehension

  • Reading comprehension
  • Vocabulary
  • Basic grammar
  • Sentence formation

2. General Hindi Comprehension

  • अपठित गद्यांश
  • शब्दावली
  • व्याकरण
  • वाक्य संरचना

3. Logical & Analytical Reasoning

  • Number series
  • Coding–decoding
  • Blood relations
  • Analogy

4. Educational & General Awareness

  • Current affairs
  • Indian education system
  • Education policies
  • General knowledge

5. Teaching-Learning & Subject Competency

  • Child development and pedagogy
  • Teaching aptitude
  • Learning processes
  • Classroom management

Qualifying Criteria

IGNOU does not announce a fixed qualifying score. Final admission depends on entrance test marks, merit ranking and seat availability at Programme Study Centres.

Important Instructions for Candidates

  • Carry admit card and valid photo ID
  • Use only blue or black ballpoint pen
  • Reach the examination centre well before reporting time

Final Words
