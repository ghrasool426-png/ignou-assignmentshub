How to Write IGNOU Assignments (2025)

Complete Step-by-Step Guide to Score Good Marks

Writing IGNOU assignments correctly is one of the most important factors in scoring good marks in Term End Examinations. Many students fail to score well not because of lack of knowledge, but due to wrong format, poor structure, copying, and ignoring IGNOU rules.

This detailed guide explains how to write IGNOU assignments step by step for all programmes including BA, MA, BSc, BBA and MSc. If you follow this guide properly, your chances of scoring high marks increase significantly.

Who Should Read This Guide?

  • New IGNOU students (1st year)
  • Students scoring low assignment marks
  • Working professionals studying via IGNOU
  • Students confused about format and evaluation

1. Understand the Assignment Question Carefully

Before writing any answer, read the assignment question multiple times. Pay attention to instruction words such as discuss, explain, analyse, describe, examine. Each word expects a different type of answer.

Writing irrelevant information is one of the biggest reasons for low marks in IGNOU assignments.

2. Follow the Correct IGNOU Assignment Format

IGNOU follows a simple handwritten assignment format unless stated otherwise. Typed assignments are usually not accepted.

  • Use A4 size paper only
  • Write neatly using blue or black pen
  • Leave proper margins on all sides
  • Write one side of the paper
  • Number all pages clearly

3. Assignment Front Page Details (Very Important)

Incorrect front page details can lead to rejection or delay in evaluation. Always include:

  • Student Name
  • Enrollment Number
  • Programme Code
  • Course Code
  • Study Centre Code
  • Session (January / July)

6. How IGNOU Assignments Are Evaluated

IGNOU assignments are evaluated by subject experts at study centres. Marks are awarded based on:

  • Relevance of answer
  • Proper format and structure
  • Original writing (no copy-paste)
  • Clarity and presentation
  • Use of examples

Poor handwriting, copied answers and wrong course codes often lead to low marks.

7. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Copying answers from internet or guides
  • Submitting previous session assignments
  • Wrong course or programme code
  • Late submission
  • Poor handwriting

8. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Is IGNOU assignment compulsory?

Yes. Without submitting assignments, you cannot appear in exams.

Can I type IGNOU assignments?

No. Assignments must be handwritten unless IGNOU specifies otherwise.

Can I copy from solved guides?

No. Copying may result in zero marks or rejection.
