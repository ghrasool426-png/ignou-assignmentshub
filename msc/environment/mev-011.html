Question 1: Explain the concept of environment and its major components.

The term environment refers to the sum total of all external conditions, influences and interactions that affect the life and development of organisms. It includes both biotic (living) and abiotic (non-living) components that surround an organism.

Environment plays a crucial role in sustaining life on Earth. It provides essential resources such as air, water, food and shelter. Human survival and development are deeply dependent on a healthy environment.

Major Components of Environment

  • Atmosphere: The gaseous layer surrounding the Earth. It contains oxygen, nitrogen and other gases necessary for life and climate regulation.
  • Hydrosphere: Includes all forms of water such as oceans, rivers, lakes, glaciers and groundwater. It supports aquatic life and regulates temperature.
  • Lithosphere: The solid outer layer of Earth consisting of soil, rocks and minerals. It provides land for agriculture and human settlements.
  • Biosphere: Comprises all living organisms including plants, animals and microorganisms interacting with other environmental components.

The balance among these components is essential for ecological stability and sustainable development.

Question 2: Discuss ecosystem structure and functions.

An ecosystem is a functional unit of nature where living organisms interact with the physical environment. It includes biotic and abiotic components functioning together through energy flow and nutrient cycling.

Structure of an Ecosystem

The structure of an ecosystem refers to the organization of its components:

  • Producers: Green plants that prepare food through photosynthesis.
  • Consumers: Herbivores, carnivores and omnivores that depend on producers.
  • Decomposers: Bacteria and fungi that break down organic matter.

Functions of an Ecosystem

  • Energy flow through food chains and food webs
  • Nutrient cycling (carbon, nitrogen, phosphorus cycles)
  • Regulation of population and climate
  • Support of biodiversity

Efficient ecosystem functioning is necessary for environmental sustainability.

Question 3: Describe natural resources and their conservation.

Natural resources are materials and components found in nature that are used by humans for survival and development. These include renewable and non-renewable resources.

Types of Natural Resources

  • Forest resources
  • Water resources
  • Mineral resources
  • Energy resources
  • Land resources

Need for Conservation

Rapid population growth, industrialization and over-exploitation have led to depletion of natural resources. Conservation ensures sustainable use for future generations.

Conservation methods include afforestation, water harvesting, renewable energy use and environmental laws.

Question 4: Explain environmental pollution and its types.

Environmental pollution refers to the introduction of harmful substances into the environment causing adverse effects on living organisms and ecosystems.

Major Types of Pollution

  • Air pollution
  • Water pollution
  • Soil pollution
  • Noise pollution
  • Radioactive pollution

Control of pollution is essential for maintaining environmental quality and human health.

Question 5: Discuss the role of environmental education.

Environmental education aims to create awareness about environmental issues and promote responsible behavior toward nature.

It helps individuals understand ecological problems, develop sustainable practices and participate in environmental protection.

Environmental education plays a key role in achieving sustainable development goals.
