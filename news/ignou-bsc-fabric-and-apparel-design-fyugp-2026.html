IGNOU Launches B.Sc Fabric and Apparel Design Programme (FYUGP)

New Four-Year Undergraduate Programme aligned with NEP 2020 | Admissions Open from January 2026

Indira Gandhi National Open University (IGNOU) has officially launched a new undergraduate programme, B.Sc Fabric and Apparel Design, under the Four-Year Undergraduate Programme (FYUGP) starting from the January 2026 admission cycle.

The programme is designed to meet the growing demand for skilled professionals in the textile, fashion, and apparel industry and follows the guidelines of the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020.

B.Sc Fabric and Apparel Design Programme Overview

The B.Sc Fabric and Apparel Design programme provides students with a balanced combination of technical knowledge, creative skills, and industry-oriented training. It focuses on fabric production, garment construction, apparel manufacturing, and fashion design.

Key Learning Areas

  • Fabric production and textile science
  • Apparel manufacturing processes
  • Garment construction and pattern making
  • Fashion design and product development
  • Quality control in apparel manufacturing
  • Supply chain and production management

Objectives of the Programme

  • To provide comprehensive understanding of the fabric and apparel industry
  • To develop practical and technical skills for apparel production
  • To train students in maintaining quality standards in clothing manufacturing
  • To encourage entrepreneurship and start-up opportunities in fashion and apparel

UGC & AIU Recognition

The B.Sc Fabric and Apparel Design programme is recognized by UGC and AIU, making it equivalent to regular university degrees. Graduates are eligible for government jobs, private sector employment, and international opportunities.

Career Opportunities After B.Sc Fabric and Apparel Design

  • Textile Designer
  • Fabric Designer
  • Fashion Designer
  • Apparel Pattern Maker
  • Technical Designer
  • Fashion Merchandiser
  • Visual Merchandiser
  • Fashion Educator
