Q1. What is Liberalism? Explain.

Liberalism is one of the most influential political ideologies that emphasizes individual freedom, equality before law, reason, tolerance and limited government. The core belief of liberalism is that individuals are rational and capable of making decisions for their own welfare, and therefore their liberty should be protected from unnecessary state interference.

Historically, liberalism emerged in Europe during the 17th and 18th centuries as a reaction against absolute monarchy, feudal privileges and the domination of the church. Thinkers like John Locke, Adam Smith, Jeremy Bentham and J.S. Mill laid its foundations. John Locke argued that individuals possess natural rights such as life, liberty and property, and the main purpose of the state is to protect these rights.

Political liberalism supports constitutional government, rule of law, separation of powers and civil liberties. Economic liberalism advocates free markets and private property. In the 20th century, modern liberalism emphasized welfare, education and social security to ensure real freedom.

Q2. Write a note on Welfare State.

A Welfare State is a form of state organization in which the government assumes responsibility for the social and economic well-being of its citizens. It aims to ensure minimum living standards, social security and equal opportunities.

The welfare state expanded in the 20th century after the Great Depression and World War II. It includes public healthcare, education, unemployment benefits, pensions and housing assistance. Though criticized for high expenditure, it remains vital for social justice.

Q3. What do you understand by Libertarianism?

Libertarianism emphasizes maximum individual liberty and minimal state interference. It supports free markets and strong property rights. Thinkers like Robert Nozick argued that redistribution through taxation violates individual freedom.

Q4. Discuss Historical Materialism.

Historical materialism, developed by Karl Marx, explains history through material conditions and class struggle. According to Marx, economic relations shape politics, law and ideology.

Q6. Explain the characteristics of Fundamentalism.

Fundamentalism stresses strict adherence to religious doctrines, rejection of secularism and intolerance of pluralism. It often resists modern democratic values.

Q7. What is Nationalism?

Nationalism emphasizes loyalty to the nation based on shared culture, history or territory. It has inspired liberation movements but also conflicts.

Q8. Elaborate upon Multiculturalism.

Multiculturalism supports cultural diversity, minority rights and inclusive citizenship. It challenges assimilation and promotes coexistence.

Q9. Explain the Fascist World View.

Fascism is an authoritarian ideology that rejects democracy and liberalism. It promotes nationalism, militarism and absolute state authority.
